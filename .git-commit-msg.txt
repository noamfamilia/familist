Record authoritative terminal outcomes for sync queue history

The queue history was inferring success/failure from the last live-query snapshot
before a sync_queue row was deleted, and treated any non-empty `last_error` as a
failure. `last_error` is never cleared between attempts, so any row that hit a
transient error (e.g. a connectivity blip during online recovery) and then
succeeded on retry was shown as `fail` even though the server-side change went
through correctly.

Move the truth into the sync engine: when the drain loop physically deletes a
row, it records the row's final outcome (`success` after `executeOutboundRow`
resolves, `failure` after `scrubAfterTerminalOutboundFailure`). The queue history
UI consumes that outcome by row id and falls back only when no outcome was
recorded — and the fallback now ignores `last_error` and uses `status === 'failed'`
as the sole failure signal.
